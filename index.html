Charles Law Applications

Charles Law states that the volume of a given mass of gas increases or decreases by 1/273 of its volume at 0 degrees celsius for each degree rise or fall in temperature, provided pressure is kept constant

Read Below For Applications...

Bakery Products

Charles Law application in real life can be seen in our kitchen too. In order to make bread and cakes soft and spongy, yeast is used for fermentation. Yeast produces carbon dioxide gas. When bread and cakes are baked at high temperatures; with an increase in temperature, carbon dioxide gas expands. As a result of this expansion, our bread and cakes become deliciously spongy and fluffy in appearance and ready to serve.

Opening of Soda Can

When we open a chilled soda can we hardly see bubbles but when we open a warm soda can the bubbles rush out. According to Charles’s law definition, in a chilled soda can, due to low temperature, there is a decrease in the volume. That’s why you hardly see any bubble coming out of the soda can. On the other hand, in a warmer soda can, due to the high temperature, there is an increase in the volume. That’s why bubbles spill out of the drink.

Helium Balloon On Cold Day

On cold or chilly days,The balloon will shrink a bit due to the degree of coldness or decrease in temperature. It happens because of Charles’s gas Law. According to Charles law definition; when the temperature decreases so do the volume of helium gas inside a balloon. On the other hand, when the same balloon is brought back to a worm room, it regains its original shape.
